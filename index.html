★ 2001 · Christianshavn

Our
Story.

Silicon Valley has the garage. Ours was an old merchant's building in Christianshavn.

Wildersgade 51. A basement room. Jacob — our founder — and a handful of people who believed they could change accounting in Denmark.

Before employees. Before 250,000 customers. Before roadmaps, strategy decks, or a category called AI or Automation.

Just the problem. A small group of people. And the idea.

The first thing they shipped was a Balance Total report. One developer built it as a safe starting point — something simple, careful, solid. It only read data.

Twenty-five years later, it's still running.

That's where e-conomic started. Not with certainty or scale. But with curiosity, momentum, and people willing to build before they had all the answers.

★ This year's mission

Back to the
Garage.

"Back to the Garage" isn't nostalgia, and it isn't mythology borrowed from California. It's a reminder that this company was built in small rooms, by small teams, doing things that did not exist yet. And now we're standing at another shift.

Paper to cloud changed everything. And we will do it again.

The tools we have today would have felt like a dream in that basement on Wildersgade. But the mindset is the same:

An idea.
A room.
People willing to try.

That's what this hackathon is about.

Not perfection.
Not polished pitches.
Not knowing everything before you start.

It's about the joy of building cool stuff. Experimenting. Learning across teams with people you've never worked with before.

Designers. Engineers. PMs. Support. Sales. Analysts — and everyone in between. One room for 24 hours.

Some ideas will fail.
Some will bite off more than they can chew.
A few might change how we work.

That's usually how new chapters begin.

You don't need to know how to code. You just need curiosity — and the courage to try.

Twenty-five years ago, a few people in Christianshavn started something with no blueprint for what came next.

Now it's our turn.

24 hours.
Here's the shape.

Pitches in the morning, building through the night, demos on the stage. Same garage energy — every team picks a 30-second anthem that plays before they take the stage.

DAY 1 Pitch & build

Ideas land, teams form, the work begins.

09:00 – 10:00

Opening & pitches

Everyone in the room. Pitchers present their ideas with a number and an image. Joiners listen and pick a team.

10:00 – 10:30

Team formation

Teams settle in and fill out the experiment card together — what are you trying to solve, the idea, how you'll test it, what success looks like.

⏰ 12:00 deadline

Experiment cards in

Every team submits their experiment card to the organisers before hacking starts.

12:00 – 17:30

Hack time

Lunch together. Snacks throughout. Organisers float between teams for check-ins. Build, design, research, code.

17:30

Fun break

Thirty minutes, everyone together. A group activity to recharge and cross-pollinate between teams.

18:00

Dinner

Catered. Casual. Team bonding over food. Some folks head home after — no pressure.

After dinner – 24:00

Late night hack ☕ (optional)

Totally optional — but if you catch the building bug, the workspace stays open until midnight. Snacks, coffee, and quiet focus for anyone who wants to push the final stretch.

DAY 2 Ship & show

Wrap the build, lock the deck, take the stage.

08:30

Arrival & breakfast

Come in late if you stayed late — no judgement. Breakfast on the table, reconnect with the team, review where you left off.

⏰ 11:00 deadline

Deliver your code

Code and prototype submitted. Final bug fixes and cleanup land before this point.

⏰ 14:00 deadline

Presentations locked

Final decks and demos delivered to the common platform. No editing after this — what you submit is what you present.

12:00 – 15:00

Free time & lunch

Breathe. The hard part is done. Eat together, practice your pitch if you want, get hyped for the afternoon.

15:15 – 17:30

Final presentations 🎤

Sharp start at 15:15. Every team takes the stage, demos their work, answers questions. We watch, we cheer, we vote, we celebrate.

Questions &
Answers.

Click a question to expand ↓
Who can join the hackathon?
Everyone at e-conomic — product, sales, WOW, finance, reach, and everyone in between. No exceptions, no job title required.
How big are teams?
Up to 5 official team members. You can also have up to 2 helpers — people who might only be available for part of the event or have limited time. Helpers won't be eligible for prizes if the team wins, but their contribution still counts.
Do I need a team or idea before I show up?
Sign up with your role and we'll help form teams so everyone gets the support they need. Not sure you know enough people? Come with your good idea — we've got your back to get some incredible people around you to make it possible. Not to mention our favourite new little buddy, Claude.
How do we submit our work?
See how far you can get it. Each team will have developers to help with the technical side, plus access to Claude throughout the day. Technical submission is locked at the 11:00 deadline. You can keep working on your presentation until the 14:00 presentation deadline. Feel free to add any additional resources to support your pitch.
Are there prizes?
We build for the joy of creating something cool in a short amount of time. There will be juries and prizes — details announced on the day. Internal judges who genuinely can't wait to see what you come up with.
Can I use AI tools?
Please do — we are amplified by AI. Something our friends in the 1980s would have loved to have. Go nuts, experiment, try something new, push the boundaries. This is an amazing playground to create, experiment, and test. Don't hold back.
What can we build?
Everyone has a good idea. A project can be many things — an AI workflow for your team, a new feature for a customer, a rethink of an existing page, embedded AI functionality. Maybe this is finally the year someone vibe codes a brand new design and layout. How many years have we been trying to make that happen at hackathon?
What if I have an idea but I'm nervous about pitching?
Sign up and reach out — we can partner you with a product person so you can pitch together. If you want to learn how to build a great 2-minute pitch (that's all you get), sign up and we'll invite you to a How to Pitch session in August. We are here to support you. Let us know what makes you nervous and we'll find a cool way to work through it.
Do I need to stay until midnight to take part?

Not at all. We want to make space for everyone to join — this is about collaboration, learning, meeting new people, and having fun. There are lots of ways to be in it, and none of them require burning the midnight oil.

Deadlines have moved to 11:00 the next morning, so you choose your own adventure:

  • Working hours only — build hard during the day and clock out guilt-free.
  • Working hours + some remote time — sneak in a few extra hours after the kids are asleep.
  • Working hours + dinner — stay for food and good vibes until 18:00.
  • Working hours + dinner + late night — brew the coffee, stay late, and see what happens when the building goes quiet.
