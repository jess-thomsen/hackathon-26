The
grain cellar.

Wildersgade 51, Christianshavn. An old merchant's building with a trap door in the floor. Jacob — our founder — and a handful of people who believed. Before there were employees, before there was funding, before there was a plan beyond the idea itself.

The first feature we ever shipped was a Balance Total report. It was written by one developer who just wanted to start with something that only reads data. Twenty-five years later. Still running.

That is what "Back to the Garage" means. Not nostalgia. Not mythology borrowed from California. Ours. You now have superpowers those first few people in Christianshavn couldn't have imagined — small team, good idea, amplified by AI. The question is what you'll build in the next 24 hours.

24 hours.
Here's the shape.

Pitches in the morning, building through the night, demos on the stage. Same garage energy — every team picks a 30-second anthem that plays before they take the stage.

DAY 1 Pitch & build

Ideas land, teams form, the work begins.

09:00 – 10:00

Opening & pitches

Everyone in the room. Pitchers present their ideas with a number and an image. Joiners listen and pick a team.

10:00 – 12:00

Team formation

Teams settle in and fill out the hypothesis card together — problem, idea, how we'll validate, what success looks like.

⏰ 12:00 deadline

Hypothesis cards in

Every team submits their hypothesis card to the organisers before hacking starts.

12:00 – 17:30

Hack time

Lunch together. Snacks throughout. Organisers float between teams for check-ins. Build, design, research, code.

17:30

Fun break

Thirty minutes, everyone together. A group activity to recharge and cross-pollinate between teams.

18:00

Dinner

Catered. Casual. Team bonding over food. Some folks head home after — no pressure.

After dinner

Late night hack ☕

Optional. Coffee brewing, workspace open. For the level-4 sign-ups who want to push the final stretch.

DAY 2 Ship & show

Wrap the build, lock the deck, take the stage.

08:30

Arrival & breakfast

Come in late if you stayed late — no judgement. Breakfast on the table, reconnect with the team, review where you left off.

⏰ 10:00 deadline

Deliver your code

Code and prototype submitted. Final bug fixes and cleanup land before this point.

⏰ 13:00 deadline

Presentations locked

Final decks and demos delivered to the common platform. No editing after this — what you submit is what you present.

12:00 – 15:00

Free time & lunch

Breathe. The hard part is done. Eat together, practice your pitch if you want, get hyped for the afternoon.

15:15 – 17:30

Final presentations 🎤

Sharp start at 15:15. Every team takes the stage, demos their work, answers questions. We watch, we cheer, we vote, we celebrate.

Questions &
Answers.

Click a question to expand ↓
Who can join the hackathon?
Anyone at e-conomic — product, design, engineering, marketing, support, finance. The grain cellar didn't have job titles either. Cross-functional teams tend to make the most interesting things, so don't wait for "your people" to sign up first.
Do I need to come with an idea?
No. We'll run an idea-pitching session at the morning kickoff. Bring a half-formed thought, a frustration, or nothing at all — you'll find a team by lunch.
How big are teams?
Two to five people. Solo entries are allowed but discouraged — the point is to work with someone you wouldn't normally work with.
What counts as "shipping"?
A working prototype people can click through, a real integration, a usable internal tool, a video of a physical thing — anything tangible. Slide decks alone don't count. Show the thing.
Are there prizes?
Yes — categories include Most Useful, Most Surprising, Best Craft, and a People's Choice vote. Prizes are deliberately silly. The real prize is bragging rights and possibly your project shipping for real.
What if I can't code?
Great. We need researchers, writers, designers, and people who can wrangle stakeholders. Some of the strongest past projects were carried by non-engineers who kept the team focused.
Can I use AI tools?
Please do. Claude, Cursor, v0, Figma Make — all fair game. We care about the outcome and the craft of taste, not which keys you pressed.
